Encryption Modes

Select one of the following methods when initiating the encryption process:

Just Files: Encrypts each selected file individually.
Just Zip: Compresses all selected files into a single ZIP archive, then performs a full-archive encryption.
Files and Zip: Encrypts files individually, archives them, and performs a final layer of encryption on the resulting ZIP.  
Advanced OptionsDouble Zip (Available for "Just Zip" and "Files and Zip"):
Encapsulates the previously encrypted archive into a new, secondary ZIP file.
Supports an optional second password to establish dual-layer encryption with distinct cryptographic keys.

Delete After Encryption:
Securely wipes the original source files from storage immediately upon completion of the encryption process.
Employs media purging techniques to prevent forensic recovery.

Security UtilitiesSafe Delete (Main Screen):
Permanently destroys files to ensure they are unrecoverable by forensic tools.
Utilizes NIST SP 800-88 compliant media sanitization.

Safe Exit:
Sanitizes the application's RAM to prevent sensitive data leakage.
Implements active compiler barriers to ensure thorough memory clearance and prevent residual remnants.

Security Notice: Password PolicyThis

application utilizes state-of-the-art, military-grade cryptographic architecture specifically engineered to withstand advanced cyber-laboratory decryption attempts.

However, the efficacy of this security model is strictly dependent on the strength of your master password.
No amount of advanced encryption can compensate for a weak or predictable password.
To maintain the integrity of your encrypted data, strictly adhere to the following guidelines:

Avoid Predictability: Do not use any information related to your personal life, such as names, dates of birth, social media usernames, or significant locations.

Defend Against Social Engineering: Your password must not be derivable through social engineering or public observation of your habits.

Use High-Entropy Passphrases: Prioritize long, complex, and completely unpredictable strings of characters. The strength of your security is directly proportional to the complexity of the password you choose.
